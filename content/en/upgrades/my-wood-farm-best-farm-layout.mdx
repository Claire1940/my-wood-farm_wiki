<Section icon={Map} title="Core Mechanics of Wood Farming in Core Keeper">

Establishing a **my wood farm best farm layout** in Core Keeper is essential for securing a constant supply of wood for crafting, building, and fueling your base operations. Unlike regular crops, wood farming relies on root seeds (such as Wood, Birch, or Coral roots) that grow into permanent seed bulbs. These bulbs act as spawning points, sending out long wood tendrils over time. 

Understanding how these tendrils behave is the first step to designing an efficient layout. Roots require specific ground conditions and ample space to spread. If you crowd your planting area, the tendrils will block each other, severely reducing your overall wood yield.

| Root Type | Required Ground | Max Growth Depth | Primary Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Wood Root** | Dirt / Turf | 6 Tiles | Torches, basic chests, tools |
| **Birch Root** | Stone | 6 Tiles | Advanced construction, handles |
| **Coral Root** | Sand | 6 Tiles | High-tier crafting, specialized items |

<Callout type="warning" title="Growth Limitations">
Root tendrils will only grow up to a maximum of six tiles away from the main seed bulb. Designing a farm wider than this limit will result in empty, wasted space.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="The Best Manual Wood Farm Layout">

Before you unlock advanced automation technologies like robotic arms and mechanical drills, you must rely on manual harvesting. A manual layout focuses on easy accessibility, allowing you to run down pathways with an axe or a high-tier sword to quickly clear out the grown wood.

The optimal manual layout utilizes a "comb" pattern. By planting seed bulbs in a central spine, you allow the roots to grow outward into dedicated harvesting lanes. This prevents the player from accidentally hitting the main seed bulbs while clearing the wood.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Automated Farm Setup">

As you progress deeper into the underground, manual harvesting becomes a tedious chore. To achieve the absolute **my wood farm best farm layout**, you must transition to a fully automated system. This system uses automated drills to break the grown wood and conveyor belts to transport the resources directly into localized storage chests.

This setup requires specific positioning to ensure the drills break only the grown tendrils while leaving the main seed bulbs completely unharmed.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear the Land and Lay the Ground">
    Clear a flat area of at least 15x15 tiles. Replace the floor tiles with the appropriate ground type for your chosen root (Dirt for regular wood).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Plant the Seed Bulbs">
    Hoe the ground and plant your root seeds in a straight line. Leave a gap of exactly five tiles between parallel rows of seed bulbs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Position the Drills">
    Place automated drills facing the paths where the root tendrils will grow. Ensure the drills are powered by electrical wiring running underneath the floor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Set Up Conveyors and Chests">
    Lay down conveyor belts behind the drills to catch the harvested wood. Route these belts to feed directly into a centralized copper or iron chest.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Component | Quantity Needed | Placement Rule | Function |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Root Seed** | 10 - 20 | Spaced 5 tiles apart | Generates the wood tendrils |
| **Automated Drill** | 1 per growth tile | Facing the growth path | Automatically breaks grown wood |
| **Conveyor Belt** | Variable | Behind/under harvest points | Transports wood to chests |
| **Electricity Wire** | Variable | Underneath machinery | Powers the automated drills |

<Callout type="success" title="Storage Optimization">
Use sorting hoppers alongside your storage chests to automatically separate wood from other agricultural products like fiber or bomb peppers.
</Callout>

</Section>
