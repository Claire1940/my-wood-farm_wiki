<Section icon={Shield} title="Plot Expansion and Layout Strategy">

Plot expansion is often misunderstood by new players. While more land seems better, it also increases the distance you must travel to sell wood. A well-designed layout is essential for maintaining a high "Cash Per Minute" (CPM) rate. In My Wood Farm, land is expensive, so every square foot must be utilized effectively.

| Expansion Stage | Cost Range | New Features | Priority |

| **Stage 1-2** | Low | Extra tree spawns | Essential |
| **Stage 3-5** | Moderate | Stump access | High |

When arranging your farm, keep your highest-tier axes near the center of the plot where the most durable trees spawn. This reduces the time the AI or your auto-choppers spend navigating. If the game allows for custom placement, group your axes in a "U" shape around the selling point to minimize the return trip.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Maximizing Offline Earnings">

One of the most powerful features in My Wood Farm is the ability to earn while you are not actively playing. This passive income is calculated based on your total axe power and the number of trees available on your plot. To make the most of this, you need to prepare your farm before every log-off.

The offline formula generally follows a simplified version of your active production:
`Total Wood = (Sum of Axe Power) x (Offline Time) x (Efficiency Multiplier)`

To increase your efficiency multiplier, you should focus on "Production Speed" upgrades in the shop. These upgrades are often cheaper than new axes but provide a percentage boost that applies to every tool you own.

| Upgrade Category | Effect on Active Play | Effect on Offline Play | ROI Rank |
|------------------|-----------------------|------------------------|----------|
| **Axe Power** | Very High | High | 1 |
| **Swing Speed** | High | High | 2 |
| **Wood Value** | Moderate | Moderate | 3 |
| **Plot Size** | Low | Moderate | 4 |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
If you know you will be away for more than 8 hours, prioritize "Wood Value" upgrades. Since offline storage can sometimes cap out, making each piece of wood worth more is better than simply producing more volume that you can't store.
</Callout>
