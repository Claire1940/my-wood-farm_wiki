### The Essential Gameplay Cycle

Every successful lumberjack follows a strict three-part routine to keep their cash flow moving.

| Phase | Core Action | Primary Objective |
|---|---|---|
| **Chop** | Strike trees with your equipped axe | Gather raw wood on your plot |
| **Sell** | Transport gathered wood to the sell zone | Convert raw materials into cash |
| **Upgrade** | Purchase stronger axes and expand land | Increase production efficiency |

<Callout type="tip" title="Efficiency Tip">
Always stand close to the trees on your own plot during active sessions. This minimizes travel time between chopping and selling, keeping your early-game cash loop as tight as possible.
</Callout>

For new players, the first 30 minutes are crucial. Spending your initial money on the wrong upgrades can slow down your progress. Follow this streamlined path to maximize your early-game efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip the Wooden Axe">
    Start by using your basic starter Wooden Axe. Walk up to the regular trees on your plot and begin chopping. Collect every piece of wood that drops.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete Initial Sales">
    Once your inventory has a decent amount of wood, head to the designated selling point. Convert your lumber into cash immediately. Do not let usable wood sit idle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Purchase Your First Axe Upgrade">
    Reinvest your first batch of cash directly into an upgraded axe. A stronger axe directly increases your damage per swing, allowing you to clear trees much faster.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy Additional Axes">
    As you earn more cash, buy extra axes to place on your plot. These automated tools will chop nearby trees independently, boosting your passive production rate.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Expand Your Plot Boundary">
    When your current plot is fully packed with working axes and you run out of space, purchase your first plot expansion to unlock more tree spawns and placement slots.
  </Step>

### How to Calculate Your Passive Yields

To track your farm's efficiency, use these simple formulas:

- **Offline Wood Gained** = Wood balance upon return - Wood balance before logging off
- **Estimated Cash Value** = Offline Wood Gained × Current sale value per unit of wood
