The loop is simple but requires strategy: chop trees to gain wood, walk to the sell point to receive cash, and use that cash to buy better equipment. To maximize your efficiency in the first session, follow this optimized route. This path ensures you don't waste cash on unnecessary expansions before your production can support them.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The First Harvest">
    Equip your starting Wooden Axe and target the closest regular trees. Do not wander far from the sell zone. Collect enough wood to reach your first 500 cash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="First Production Boost">
    Instead of buying more land, go to the upgrade shop and purchase the first available Axe Speed upgrade. This reduces the number of swings required per tree, effectively doubling your early income.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Automation Phase">
    Purchase a second axe slot. Placing two axes on a single plot allows you to clear trees twice as fast, which is essential for maintaining the production loop while you are briefly away from the screen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Targeting Stumps">
    Once you afford a Rare-tier axe, move your focus from regular trees to Tree Stumps. While they take longer to clear, the wood yield per second is significantly higher, making your sell trips more profitable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Strategic Expansion">
    Only buy your second plot of land once your first plot is fully occupied with upgraded axes. Expansion increases the "travel time" to the sell zone, so you need high-speed production to compensate for the distance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Tree Types and Resource Values">

Not all wood is created equal in My Wood Farm. As you progress, you will encounter different tree types that act as "skill checks" for your current equipment. If a tree takes more than 30 seconds to clear, your axe is likely under-leveled for that target.

| Tree Target | Durability | Wood Yield | Recommended Axe |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Regular Tree** | Low | Standard | Wooden / Basic Rare |
| **Tree Stump** | High | 3x Standard | Rare / Legendary |
| **Giant Tree** | Very High | 10x Standard | Legendary / Magical |
| **Dead Wood** | Very Low | Minimal | Any (Clear for space) |

Understanding the durability-to-reward ratio is key. Giant Trees offer the most wood, but if your axe is too weak, you are better off clearing three regular trees in the same amount of time. 

<Callout type="info" title="Resource Management">
The selling value of wood scales slightly as you unlock new areas, but the primary way to increase cash flow is through volume. More wood per second is always better than trying to find "higher value" wood.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Offline Earnings and FAQ">

One of the best features of My Wood Farm is the ability to earn while you sleep. Your axes will continue to harvest wood based on their last assigned positions. To maximize this, always ensure your highest-tier axes are assigned to the most durable trees (like Giant Trees or Stumps) before you log off. This prevents the axes from "running out" of targets quickly.

<Callout type="tip" title="Offline Strategy">
Before logging out, do one final sell and spend all your cash on permanent upgrades. Cash sitting in your inventory doesn't help you grow, but a faster axe will earn you more wood while you are away.
</Callout>
